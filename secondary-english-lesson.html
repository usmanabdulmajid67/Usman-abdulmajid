Parts of Speech: Nouns and Pronouns

In English, nouns are words that name people, places, things, or ideas. Pronouns are words used instead of nouns to avoid repetition.

Types of Nouns

  • Common Nouns: General names (e.g., cat, city, book)
  • Proper Nouns: Specific names (e.g., Usman, London, Africa)
  • Abstract Nouns: Ideas or qualities (e.g., freedom, love)

Pronouns

Pronouns replace nouns to make sentences simpler:

  • He, She, It, They
  • I, You, We

Exercise: Identify Nouns and Pronouns

Fill in the blanks with the correct noun or pronoun:

  1. ___ is reading a book. (Use a pronoun)
  2. The ___ is big. (Name a place)
  3. ___ like freedom and peace. (Name an idea)
